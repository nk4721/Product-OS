# Product Playbook

## Purpose

This playbook provides practical rules and templates for running product work consistently across Product OS.

## Start With a Decision

Before assigning work, define the decision the work should enable. Examples include whether to investigate, build, price, pilot, release, scale, or stop an initiative.

## Idea Brief

Every new initiative starts with:

- Working title.
- Source of the idea.
- Customer or business problem.
- Affected user or segment.
- Expected customer and business value.
- Evidence already available.
- Assumptions and unknowns.
- Strategic relevance.
- Proposed next decision.

## Discovery Standard

Discovery should answer:

1. Who experiences the problem?
2. What are they trying to achieve?
3. How often and how severely does the problem occur?
4. How is it solved today?
5. What is unsatisfactory about the current solution?
6. What evidence would disprove the opportunity?
7. Is the problem important enough to change behaviour or pay?

Do not report interview count as success. Report what changed in the team's understanding and decisions.

## Business Case Standard

A business case must include:

- Problem and target segment.
- Value proposition.
- Strategic fit.
- Alternatives and competition.
- Commercial model and pricing hypothesis.
- Revenue and cost scenarios with labelled assumptions.
- Delivery, operational, legal, privacy, and adoption risks.
- KPIs and leading indicators.
- Smallest responsible validation investment.
- Proceed, pivot, park, or stop recommendation.

## Product Requirement Standard

Requirements must describe outcomes rather than only features. Include user need, scope, non-goals, user journey, acceptance criteria, data and integration needs, telemetry, security, accessibility, dependencies, and rollout plan.

## Prioritisation

Evaluate initiatives using:

- Customer impact.
- Strategic alignment.
- Evidence strength.
- Commercial value.
- Risk reduction or learning value.
- Delivery effort and operational cost.
- Urgency and dependencies.

A high score does not replace judgement. Record the reasoning and trade-offs.

## Experiment Standard

Every experiment defines a hypothesis, target group, intervention, success and failure thresholds, duration, required data, ethical or privacy constraints, and the decision that follows each possible result.

## Delivery Standard

- Build the smallest increment that creates learning or customer value.
- Keep changes reviewable and reversible.
- Add tests and observability with the feature.
- Update documentation and decisions as part of delivery.
- Avoid hidden manual operations that cannot scale or be supported.

## Release Standard

A release requires:

- Acceptance criteria met.
- Definition of Done satisfied.
- Known defects assessed.
- Security and privacy checks complete.
- Monitoring and rollback ready.
- User and operational documentation ready.
- Customer Success and Sales Enablement prepared where relevant.
- Product Director approval.

## Product Metrics

Use a balanced measurement set:

- **Value:** customer outcome or time saved.
- **Adoption:** activation, active use, feature penetration.
- **Retention:** continued use and renewal signals.
- **Quality:** defects, reliability, performance, support burden.
- **Commercial:** conversion, revenue, margin, expansion.
- **Learning:** assumptions validated or rejected and decision speed.

Avoid metrics that reward surveillance or raw activity without demonstrating value.

## Post-Launch Review

Review expected versus actual customer behaviour, KPI movement, incidents, support demand, commercial performance, qualitative feedback, incorrect assumptions, and recommended actions. Feed learnings back into discovery and update Product OS where the process itself can improve.

## Documentation Rules

- Use the paths and owners in `docs/document-owners.md`.
- Link to the source of truth rather than duplicating information.
- Separate facts, evidence, assumptions, decisions, and open questions.
- Date material decisions and identify the responsible owner.
- Archive outdated documents and link to their replacement.
